What is a Forum?

A forum is an online discussion group. Online services and bulletin board services (BBS's) provide a variety of forums, in which participants with common interests can exchange open messages.

Newsgroups versus Conferences

Forums are sometimes called newsgroups or conferences. The terms are only partly synonymous. Newsgroups support asynchronous communication; conferences are a feature of instant messaging. The latter is not a good option for system development forums, for three reasons:

  • System development forums must support discussions that span days, weeks, or months.
  • An instant messaging conference is like a meeting; getting the timezone-dispersed members of an open-source project online at once can be difficult.
  • Finally, system development projects need persistent records of their discussions.

Forum Organization

Forums are most useful when they are organized into topical categories. The index provides focus for both writers and readers.

The forum index from the Open Source Metaverse Project is offered here as a reasonable start for a discussion about the Phoenix Project forum index.

Forum Index of the Open Source Metaverse Project
Daily Progress Post the progress o' the day.
Milestones Post the milestones yet to be reached, and, of course, send a shout out when you reach 'em.
Tasks Tasks available.
Ideas and Such Post ideas. This could be feature suggestions, code, whatever.
General Phoenix Discussion Discuss OSMP generally. Duh.
Technical Issues Got some? Spew them here.
Defect Reports What defects don't we know about? Post them here. DO NOT POST HERE UNLESS YOU'RE SURE THERE'S NOTHING ON IT IN THE KNOWN BUG FORUM!
Known Bug and How to Fix Them We know it's not perfect. Here's where we tell you. Also good for brainstorming on how to get around bugs.

Moderated or Unmoderated?

Newsgroups are sometimes monitored by an individual or a group (the moderator) who has the authority to block messages deemed inappropriate. Moderated newsgroups have fewer flames and less spam than unmoderated forums.

Of course, moderating a forum takes somebody's time, time that could be spent on some other part of the project.

Technology

The old internet newsgroups are pretty much passé. Popular alternatives are:

  • mailing lists — This is effectively what is currently used by the Phoenix Project. It suffers from the lack of persistence and organization.
  • Wiki — Forums can be placed within a Wiki, which could also serve for shared document content management.
  • bulletin board service — The Metaverse Project uses phpBB, a free, open-source, bulletin board package. The Phoenix Project could host phpBB, or a competing package, on the project's web site.
