How to label materials submitted to this project

We wish to clarify the process by which we place our material into the public domain so that others can use it. As part of that process, we place the material shown below at the bottom of every webpage and document (eg. pdf) after two blank lines, and under the title About This Page. using Ellis S. Nolley as an example person and 17 April 2004 as an example date. For simplicity, we place it on every document, regardless of regardless of whether it contains an invention.

The first and last sentences are separated from the middle four sentences by a line break. At the first usage of the person's name in the first line, we link their name to their address and 'Phoenix' and the first few words of the page title separated by the 'underscore' character, so that the author can identify from where an email came, as in the following example: mailto:e.nolley@ieee.org?subject=Phoenix_how_to_label . Optionally, after this material, you may place the centered line 'Back to the Phoenix Home Page' and link it to the home page.
