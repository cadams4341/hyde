iHealth Wearable Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitor

Announced at 2014 CES (International Consumer Electronics Show), 6 January 2014

iHealth Wearable Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitor
(Credit: iHealth)

Description provided by iHealth:

  1. Worn inside a vest
  2. Can be set to obtain blood pressure readings over an extended period of time at regular intervals of 15, 30, 45, 60 or 120 minutes
  3. Uses a rechargeable battery
  4. Stores up to 200 measurements (4+ days @ 30 minutes per measurement)
  5. Connects to:
    • Mobile device via Bluetooth (compatible with both iOS and select Android devices)
    • PC through a USB cable

Guesses, because the author was not at the show:

Based on the way the iHealth Blood Pressure Dock (Model BP3) and iHealth Wireless Blood Pressure Monitor (Model BP5) are described by the company, the author is guessing that the package would include:
  1. Upper-arm cuff
  2. Flexible air tube to transmit pressure from cuff to monitor (see model BP3, below)
  3. Vest to hold the monitor, and with partial sleeves to support the cuff

The wireless technology embedded within the device is probably derived from that used in model BP5; see below.

The device most likely employs the oscillometric blood pressure measurement technique, as do the other iHealth blood pressure monitors.

Whereas most ABPM manufacturers target their products for the clinical market, iHealth seems focused on the consumer retail market. The product is expected to be priced accordingly.

Given that inexpensive ABPMs cost in the range of US$250-US$300, and given that iHealth's non-ambulatory predecessors (see below) each cost US$100, the author suspects the iHealth ABPM would retail for about US$125-US$150.
